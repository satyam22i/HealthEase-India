Rift Valley fever

Rift Valley fever (RVF) is a viral disease primarily affecting animals but can also infect humans. It is transmitted by mosquitoes and can cause flu-like symptoms, liver abnormalities, and, in severe cases, hemorrhagic fever, encephalitis, or blindness.

Scrub Typhus

Scrub typhus is a potentially serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium Orientia tsutsugamushi, transmitted through the bite of infected chiggers (mite larvae). It typically presents with fever, rash, and eschar (a dark, scab-like area at the bite site), and is common in rural Asia and the Pacific Islands.
